What is Female Circumcision?

Female Circumcision in other words (Female Gential Mutilation) has continuously been in practice as a control of female sexuality in cultures. The partial or total removal of the female’s external genitalia that generally has been an element of a rite of passage preparing young girls for womanhood and marriage questions the ethical implications of this procedure performed on women’s bodies.

Despite the severe risk and the consequences of health and well-being on the women’s body, the practice of female circumcision continues to still present as a cultural and ethical identity in many communities as a religious obligation.




Exploring the Complexities of Cultural Ethics in Relation to Female Genital Mutilation (FGM)

Female Genital Mutilation is a continual norms for many cultures and countries as an cultural obligation for many reasons. The reasons listed include:
- maintaining martial fidelity
-controlling female sex drive
- ensuring paternity


In general, female circumcision is viewed in culture as a way to controlling female's sexual behavior and be faithful to their partners.

In many cultures, a woman's adherence to the practice of circumcised is closely tied to her reputation and social status. If they failed to comply, it means they become a social pariah, and lose all chance of having a husband.

How prevalent is FGM?

1 in 3 girls aged 15-19 living in 31 countries have undergone female genital mutilation (FGM)

The prevalent of FGM varies widely among countries and community and the exact data remains unknown however...
